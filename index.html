Enter your grades here: everything else in this tool is worked out from what you type into this one table. Start here if this is your first time using this tool.

No academic state yet. Fill in the highlighted cells below to begin: every other tab is built from this table.

What grades do I need?

Pick how many classes you have left and what GPA you're aiming for: see the simplest combination of grades that gets you there, for every combination of the two at once. Hover any cell for the full breakdown.

Just tell me the number

One target GPA, one number of classes left, one straight answer: no grid to read, no other tab needed first.

How many classes should I take?

For one target GPA, see how your effort and your real-world odds change as you take on more or fewer classes this semester, and which class count is provably the smart choice.

Compare a few options

Line up two or three class-count options against the same target GPA and see how they really differ: not just whether each one works, but how doable, sturdy, likely, and risky each one is.

My best and worst case

The best possible GPA and the worst possible GPA you could end up with, given how many classes you have left: the honest extremes, no guesswork.

How soon, or how far?

Aiming high: the fewest classes before a goal becomes possible at all. Worried about falling: how many classes of sustained bad luck it would take before a low target becomes a real risk. Both questions, one chart.

How bad could it realistically get?

A realistic worst case for a target GPA of your choice: not the theoretical extreme, and not a falsely comforting average either. Includes the full spread of possible outcomes and a few named scenarios (normal, hard, excellent, worst semester).

How much flexibility do I have left?

An actual count of how many genuinely different outcomes are still realistically open to you as you take on more classes: not just a vague sense of uncertainty.

Update my expectations with real data

Starts from a guess about your typical performance, then updates that guess using your actual grades, semester by semester, the way your own expectations naturally would.

Aim smart, not just high

For a fixed number of classes left, see which nearby GPA targets take the least wasted effort to reach, and which ones would need more than they're really worth.

Where do I stand against named bands?

See exactly what's needed for classifications like First Class Honours, or whether you're already safely past the minimum to graduate. Bands are editable, not fixed facts: defaults are commonly-cited figures, so confirm against your own faculty handbook.

Try a hypothetical semester

See what a pretend semester would do to your GPA: without touching your real, saved grades.

Was my path the only way?

See how many other ways your semesters could have played out and still added up to the exact same overall grades and the exact same number of classes per semester.

My best plan, semester by semester

Not just the smartest choice for next semester: the best sequence of choices across every semester you have left, each one already accounting for what comes after it.

The easiest number of classes to take

See how the cheapest nearby GPA target and the most convenient one both shift as you change how many classes you're taking this semester.

Look up a word, or find the right tab

Plain definitions for every term used elsewhere in this tool, what each tab actually does, the assumptions in force everywhere, and one fully worked example.
